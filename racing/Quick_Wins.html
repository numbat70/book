    Quick Wins

    Quick Wins Summary

    • Breathe
    • Look where you are going
    • Use your whole body and not just your arms
    • Pull the paddle in a straight line (to make you paddle straighter)
    • Keep the board smooth through the water (bouncing is slow)
    • Paddle more strokes per minute
    • Count your paddle strokes to calm your mind down
    • Prepare some personal motivation phrases for when things go wrong
    • Know your goals and be in touch with your motivation for being here

    If you want to get faster at racing it is quite simple:

    Breathe

    Breathe in and out every stroke. If that is too fast then do every 1.5 strokes or every 2 strokes. Really concentrate on Breathing Out. Breathing in is the easy part

    Look where you are going:

    This will make you paddle a straighter course

    Use your whole body and not just your arms

    This include dropping your body weight onto the  paddle at the catch and using it as an advantage

    Pull the paddle in a straight line:

    Don't follow the curve of the boards rail

    Smooth the board out:

    Don't bounce up and down or rock from side to side too much.

    Paddle more strokes per minute:

    More strokes means more speed.

    It is not about pulling the blade harder. All the quick wins are on the Exit, Recovery, Reach and Catch.

    "So you know that nice slow bit you do to recover in between each stroke....That needs to speed up....A LOT"

    Count your strokes when things get tough.

    It takes your mind off of things

    It also helps with a nice smooth rhythm

    Identify when things go wrong and have some pre-made thoughts lined up

    If you always fade in the middle of the race, then prepare for this.

    Prepare whatever motivational words you need to get you through.

    Know your Goals

    • Why does this race matter
    • Why are you doing it
    • If it matters enough, then you can motivate yourself
    • If it does not, then chill a little, and just enjoy a slightly slower race.

    If you are willing to train a little then:

    Learn to paddle in a really straight line

    Perfect your catch to optimise reach whilst still entering the water at a positive angle and with no splash.

    Trying to get the blade in the  water quicker will almost automatically do this for you.
