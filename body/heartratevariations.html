    Heart Rate Causes

    Heart Rate Alarm Bells

    • Almost everything makes your Heart Rate go up
    • Dropping Core Body Temperature makes it Go Down
    • This is an early indicator of potential Hypothermia
      • Watch for an abnormally dropping heart rate on longer paddles in cold weather
      • Watch for it even in average weather as other factors can still lower your core temp.
    • If your heart rate soars then you may well be Dehydrated, tired, too hot internally, too cold externally or just maxed out on Caffeine

    Here are some odd things to remember when training or racing

    When you get tired your heart rate goes up

    • This is probably the most known reason for heart rate increases. Only learning how your body responds to exercise, will allow you to understand if one of these other changes is also happening.

    When you dehydrate your heart rate will increase

    • Dehydration reduces the amount of blood in your system and the heart beats faster to try and compensate.
    • This stresses the heart unduly.
    • Just one more reason to not dehydrate.

    Hot weather will stress your heart out

    • For every degree your body’s internal temperature rises, the heart will beat roughly 10 beats per minute faster.
    • Blood pumping through your veins is the bodies methods of radiating heat.
    • Perspiring will also strain the heart and raise your heart rate.
    • Varying your internal body temperature is never a good thing.

    When you let your internal body temp drop heart rate goes down

    • This is the opposite of heating up.
    • For ever degree you lower your core temperature, your heart will slow 10 beets per minute. BUT
    • This does not refer to cold days. This is just letting your core temperature drop on an average day. Dehydration and Fatigue can play as big a part on this as can the weather.

    If you let your skin get cold then your blood vessels will shrink and this will increase your heart rate

    • This is different from Internal Temperature. It is mostly hands and head at first.
    • Cold weather will shrink your blood vessels which raises the blood pressure.
    • Your heart then has to beat faster to force blood around your system.

    Caffeine will make your heart rate go up

    • It is another Vasoconstrictor (constricts blood vessels)
    • It is common to find caffeine in:
      • Energy drinks
      • Energy Chews
      • Chew Bars
      • Coke and other fizzy drinks
      • Coffee (really?)
      • Tea
      • Chocolate
      • Ice Cream
      • Supplements
      • Pain relief tablets
      • And lots of other unexpected sources

    What this all means

    • Your heart rate can vary for a lot of reasons over and above athletic effort.
    • Learn how your heart responds to these situations if:
      • You are using your heart rate to track training effect
      • You are using your heart rate to diagnose body events (dehydration, core temp changes etc...)
      • You are using heart rate to track fatique

    Heart Rate Variability (HRV)

    • It is also worth noting that Heart Rate Variability goes down when fatiqued. This respresents the Hearts lowered ability to quickly respond to the bodies changing needs.
    • Normally, no two heart beats are identical, but as you get more tired, the "variability" decreases.
